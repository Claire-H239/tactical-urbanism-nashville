Shelby Hills & Kirkpatrick Center Tactical Urbanism Improvements

A temporary, community-led safety pilot in East Nashville to improve crossings, calm turning speeds, and support vulnerable users—especially children—near the Kirkpatrick Community Center and KIPP Kirkpatrick Elementary School.

Where: S 9th St + Heights Dr + Sevier St + S 10th St, plus Sevier St near KIPP
When: [Install date] – [End date]
What: crosswalks • painted bulb-outs • visibility/parking changes • delineators • mural

Why this project

This location is a community hub with heavy use by vulnerable users, particularly children. NDOT has identified the area as highly vulnerable, and it connects to a pedestrian high-injury corridor on Shelby Avenue.

What we’re installing

  • Marked crosswalks in key crossing locations.
  • Painted curb extensions (bulb-outs) to reduce turning speeds on/off S 10th Street.
  • Visibility improvements (including parking restrictions) near KIPP Kirkpatrick Elementary.
  • Flexible delineators mounted with butyl adhesive pads to reinforce the temporary geometry.
  • Street mural (~3,000 sq ft) to increase visibility and strengthen neighborhood identity.
  • ADA temporary curb ramp to maintain accessibility during the pilot.

This is a temporary pilot designed to be removable with minimal pavement impact.

How we’ll measure impact

We will work with NDOT to deploy a LiDAR trailer before and during implementation to collect quantitative data, paired with community surveys to gather qualitative feedback.

Timeline

  • Implementation day: setup + traffic control, cleanup, layout/marking, delineator install, mural work.
  • Observation period: LiDAR + surveys + site checks.
  • Removal day: remove delineators, remove paint/art as directed, cleanup and return materials.
