Shelby Hills & Kirkpatrick Center Tactical Urbanism Improvements

A temporary, community-led safety pilot in East Nashville to improve crossings, calm turning speeds, and support vulnerable users—especially children—near the Kirkpatrick Community Center and KIPP Kirkpatrick Elementary School.

Where: S 9th St + Heights Dr + Sevier St + S 10th St, plus Sevier St near KIPP
When: Installation: Feb 21, 2026 • Pilot period: TBD
What: crosswalks • painted bulb-outs • visibility/parking changes • delineators • mural

Why this project

This location is a community hub with heavy use by vulnerable users, particularly children. NDOT has identified the area as highly vulnerable, and it connects to a pedestrian high-injury corridor on Shelby Avenue.

What we’re installing

  • Marked crosswalks in key crossing locations.
  • Painted curb extensions (bulb-outs) to reduce turning speeds on/off S 10th Street.
  • Visibility improvements (including parking restrictions) near KIPP Kirkpatrick Elementary.
  • Flexible delineators mounted with butyl adhesive pads to reinforce the temporary geometry.
  • Street mural (~3,000 sq ft) to increase visibility and strengthen neighborhood identity.
  • ADA temporary curb ramp to maintain accessibility during the pilot.
  • Project information signage explaining the pilot and how to share feedback.

This is a temporary pilot designed to be removable with minimal pavement impact.

How we’ll measure impact

We will work with NDOT to deploy a LiDAR trailer before and during implementation to collect quantitative data, paired with community surveys to gather qualitative feedback.

Volunteer roles

No prior experience is required. Volunteers will be assigned a role on-site and supported by project leads. Tasks are designed to be safe, straightforward, and team-based.

Setup & materials

  • Unload and stage materials (cones, tape, tools, paint supplies)
  • Help set up work zones and staging areas
  • General logistics support throughout the day

Street painting support

  • Assist with surface prep (sweeping and light cleaning)
  • Apply primer/base coats where needed
  • Roll or brush paint within taped boundaries

Delineators & layout

  • Help measure and mark locations (chalk/string line)
  • Place and align bases and posts as directed by leads
  • Quality check spacing and straightness

Note: Delineators are installed using adhesive pads (no drilling).

Cleanup & closeout

  • Remove tape and dispose of waste properly
  • Return tools/materials to staging area
  • Final sweep and site walk-through with leads

Safety note: Volunteers should follow instructions from project leads and stay within designated work areas. PPE will be provided as needed.

What to expect on installation day

Installation Day is organized in short phases so volunteers can plug in where needed and breaks are built in. You can participate for the full day or for a smaller shift.

  • Check-in & orientation: Sign in, receive a quick safety briefing, and get assigned a role.
  • Work zone setup: Cones and signage are placed to define work areas and keep volunteers safe.
  • Layout & marking: Teams measure and mark crosswalks, curb extensions, and mural boundaries.
  • Installation & painting: Teams handle surface prep, taping, painting, and placing delineators.
  • Quality check: Leads verify alignment, visibility, and cleanliness before reopening areas.
  • Cleanup & closeout: Tape removal, trash collection, and a final site walk-through.

What to bring: Closed-toe shoes, clothes you don’t mind getting paint on, water, and sun/rain protection depending on weather.

What we provide: Supplies, tools, and PPE as needed (e.g., gloves, safety vests).
